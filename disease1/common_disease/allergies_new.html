Allergies

Allergies occur when the body's immune system reacts to substances that are typically harmless to most people. These substances are known as allergens and can trigger reactions such as sneezing, itching, swelling, and in severe cases, anaphylaxis.

Causes and Risk Factors

The exact cause of allergies is not always clear, but several factors can contribute to the development of allergies, including:

  • Genetic predisposition: Family history of allergies.
  • Environmental factors: Exposure to allergens like pollen, dust, mold, pet dander, etc.
  • Weakened immune system due to other medical conditions.
  • Air pollution or chemical irritants.

Symptoms

The symptoms of allergies can vary widely but commonly include:

  • Itchy or watery eyes.
  • Runny or blocked nose.
  • Hives or skin rashes.
  • Breathing difficulties or wheezing.

Treatment Options

Treatment for allergies aims to relieve symptoms and may include:

Medications:
  • Antihistamines: To reduce symptoms like itching and sneezing.
  • Corticosteroids: To reduce inflammation and swelling.
  • Decongestants: To relieve nasal congestion.
Allergy Shots:
  • Immunotherapy to desensitize the body to allergens.
Avoidance of Triggers:
  • Staying away from known allergens.
  • Maintaining clean air in the living space.

Prevention Tips

While allergies cannot always be prevented, there are measures to minimize exposure to allergens:

  • Use air purifiers to reduce indoor allergens.
  • Regularly clean bedding and carpets to remove dust mites.
  • Limit outdoor activities during high pollen seasons.
