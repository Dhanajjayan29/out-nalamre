Astigmatism

Astigmatism is a refractive error in the eye that causes blurred vision. It occurs when the cornea or lens of the eye is irregularly shaped, preventing light from focusing properly on the retina.

Causes and Risk Factors

Astigmatism is commonly caused by an irregular shape of the cornea or lens, which can lead to the following:

  • Genetics - Astigmatism can be inherited from parents.
  • Eye injury or surgery that affects the shape of the cornea.
  • Certain eye conditions such as keratoconus.

Risk factors include:

  • Family history of astigmatism.
  • Previous eye surgeries or injuries.
  • Presence of other refractive errors such as nearsightedness or farsightedness.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of astigmatism include:

  • Blurred or distorted vision at all distances.
  • Eye strain or discomfort.
  • Headaches.
  • Difficulty seeing clearly at night.

Treatment Options

The treatment for astigmatism may include:

Eyeglasses and Contact Lenses:
  • Corrective lenses are the most common treatment for astigmatism, which helps focus light properly on the retina.
Surgical Options:
  • Laser surgery (LASIK) may be recommended for reshaping the cornea in some cases of astigmatism.
  • Other procedures like toric lens implants may be suggested if eyeglasses or contacts do not work effectively.

Prevention Tips

There are no specific ways to prevent astigmatism, but the following can help protect eye health:

  • Get regular eye exams to detect refractive errors early.
  • Wear protective eyewear during activities that pose a risk to your eyes.
  • Maintain a healthy diet that supports eye health, including foods rich in vitamin A and omega-3 fatty acids.
