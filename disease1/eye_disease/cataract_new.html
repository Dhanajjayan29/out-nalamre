Cataract

Cataract is a clouding of the lens in the eye that leads to a decrease in vision. It can affect one or both eyes and is most commonly caused by aging, but can also result from injury, certain medications, or other eye conditions.

Causes and Risk Factors

Cataracts are most commonly caused by the aging process, but can also result from:

  • Age - The risk of cataracts increases with age.
  • Eye injury or surgery that affects the lens.
  • Medications such as corticosteroids can increase the risk of cataracts.

Risk factors include:

  • Family history of cataracts.
  • Excessive exposure to sunlight.
  • Diabetes or other chronic health conditions.
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of cataracts include:

  • Blurred or cloudy vision.
  • Difficulty seeing at night or in low-light conditions.
  • Double vision in one eye.
  • Fading or yellowing of colors.

Treatment Options

The treatment for cataracts may include:

Eyeglasses and Contact Lenses:
  • In the early stages, corrective lenses may improve vision. However, this will not stop the progression of cataracts.
Surgical Options:
  • Cataract surgery is the most effective treatment. It involves removing the cloudy lens and replacing it with a clear artificial lens (IOL).

Prevention Tips

Although cataracts cannot always be prevented, the following can help reduce the risk:

  • Wear sunglasses that block UV light to protect your eyes from sun damage.
  • Quit smoking and reduce alcohol consumption.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet and regular exercise.
