Bone-Joint Diseases

Osteoarthritis

Osteoarthritis is a common joint disorder characterized by the degeneration of cartilage and the underlying bone in one or more joints. It typically develops gradually.

View more

Rheumatoid arthritis

Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disorder that primarily affects the joints. In this condition, the body's immune system mistakenly attacks the synovium, the lining of the joints.

View more

Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis is a condition characterized by weakening of the bones, making them fragile and more susceptible to fractures. It occurs when the body loses too much bone or makes too little.

View more

Gout

Gout is a type of arthritis characterized by sudden, severe pain, redness, and swelling in the joints, particularly in the big toe. It is caused by the accumulation of uric acid crystals in the joints.

View more

Bone Cancer Sarcoma

Bone cancer sarcoma refers to cancer that begins in the cells of the bones. This rare and aggressive form of cancer can affect any bone in the body, with common types including osteosarcoma and Ewing's sarcoma. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment.
