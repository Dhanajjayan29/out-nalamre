NIDDM - Non-Insulin Dependent Diabetes Mellitus

NIDDM (Non-Insulin Dependent Diabetes Mellitus), also known as Type 2 diabetes, is a chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose).

Causes and Risk Factors

NIDDM develops when the body becomes resistant to insulin or when the pancreas fails to produce enough insulin. The following are key causes and risk factors:

  • Genetic factors (family history of diabetes).
  • Being overweight or obese, which can lead to insulin resistance.
  • Lack of physical activity.
  • Age (the risk increases as people age, especially after 45 years).
  • High blood pressure and high cholesterol levels.
  • Gestational diabetes during pregnancy.
  • Unhealthy diet high in processed foods and sugary drinks.

Symptoms

The symptoms of NIDDM may develop slowly, and they include:

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination.
  • Fatigue or weakness.
  • Blurred vision.
  • Slow-healing sores or infections.
  • Unexplained weight loss.
  • Tingling or numbness in the hands or feet.

Treatment Options

NIDDM treatment focuses on managing blood sugar levels and preventing complications. Treatment options include:

For Mild NIDDM:
  • Changes in lifestyle, including a healthy diet and regular physical activity.
  • Monitoring blood sugar levels regularly.
  • Weight loss and stress management techniques.
For Advanced NIDDM:
  • Oral medications, such as metformin or sulfonylureas, to help control blood sugar.
  • Insulin therapy if blood sugar is not controlled with oral medications alone.
  • Regular check-ups with healthcare professionals to monitor for complications.

Prevention Tips

To prevent NIDDM or delay its onset, consider these tips:

  • Maintain a healthy weight and body mass index (BMI).
  • Engage in regular physical activity (e.g., 30 minutes of exercise most days of the week).
  • Eat a balanced diet, focusing on whole grains, lean proteins, and fresh vegetables.
  • Avoid sugary drinks and limit processed foods.
  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly, especially if at risk.
