Eczema

Overview

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a common skin condition characterized by inflammation, redness, itching, and the formation of small, fluid-filled blisters. It is a chronic condition that tends to flare up periodically and can affect people of all ages, often starting in childhood.

Causes

  • Genetics: Eczema tends to run in families, indicating a genetic predisposition affecting the skin's barrier function.
  • Immune System Dysfunction: An overactive immune response can trigger inflammation, leading to symptoms.
  • Skin Barrier Dysfunction: A compromised skin barrier allows irritants and allergens to penetrate, causing flare-ups.
  • Environmental Triggers: Factors like harsh chemicals, allergens, dry air, and temperature changes can exacerbate symptoms.
  • Allergies: Associated conditions like asthma and hay fever can worsen eczema.
  • Microbial Factors: Infections can aggravate symptoms, with bacteria like Staphylococcus aureus commonly involved.

Symptoms

  • Dry, Itchy Skin: Persistent itching is a common and bothersome symptom.
  • Red or Inflamed Skin: Affected areas may appear reddish and feel warm to the touch.
  • Rash or Discolored Patches: Eczema can cause rough, scaly patches, often on the face, hands, and behind the knees.
  • Blisters or Oozing Lesions: Fluid-filled blisters may form and ooze during severe flare-ups.
  • Cracked or Crusty Skin: Cracking and crusting can occur, increasing infection risk.
  • Thickened or Leathery Skin: Chronic scratching may lead to skin thickening.

Treatment: Modern Medicine

  • Emollients: Moisturizers to hydrate and reduce skin dryness.
  • Topical Steroids: Steroid creams reduce inflammation and itching.
  • Paste Bandages and Wet Wraps: Used for severe itching.
  • Sedating Antihistamines: May aid in sleep but do not relieve itching directly.
  • Phototherapy: Controlled UV light exposure can reduce symptoms.
  • Oral Steroids and Immunosuppressants: Used for severe cases with caution due to side effects.

Treatment: Traditional Medicine

  • Panchakarma Therapy: An Ayurvedic detox involving medicated ghee, massages, and other treatments.
  • Herbal Remedies: Herbs like Neem, Turmeric, and Yashtimadhu offer anti-inflammatory effects.
  • Dietary Changes: A plant-based diet rich in essential nutrients helps reduce symptoms.

Caution

  • Allergens: Avoid pollen, pet dander, and mold, as they can trigger flare-ups.
  • Irritants: Harsh soaps and certain fabrics can worsen symptoms.
  • Climate: Cold or dry weather may exacerbate eczema.
  • Infections: Scratching can lead to bacterial or viral infections.

Prevention

  • Avoid known triggers and allergens.
  • Keep skin clean and moisturized.
  • Limit shower time and use warm water instead of hot.
