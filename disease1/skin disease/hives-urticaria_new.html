Hives (Urticaria)

Overview

Hives, also known as urticaria, is a skin condition characterized by the sudden onset of raised, red, and itchy welts or wheals. These welts can vary in size and often appear and disappear quickly. Hives can be acute or chronic, and while usually harmless, they can be uncomfortable and may indicate an underlying issue.

Causes

  • Allergic Reactions: Triggered by certain foods, medications, insect stings, or environmental factors.
  • Non-Allergic Causes: Infections, stress, autoimmune disorders, or extreme temperatures can also cause hives.
  • Idiopathic Urticaria: In some cases, the cause remains unknown.
  • Acute Urticaria: Lasts a few hours to weeks, often related to identifiable triggers.
  • Chronic Urticaria: Persists for over six weeks, with harder-to-identify causes.

Symptoms

  • Severe Itching: Intense itching in affected areas.
  • Painful Swelling: Known as angioedema, this can affect lips and throat.
  • Red or Skin-Colored Welts: Sudden appearance of raised patches.
  • Inflammation: Immune response causing skin inflammation.

Treatment: Modern Medicine

  • Antihistamines: Help block histamine effects, reducing itching and swelling.
  • Corticosteroids: Prescribed to reduce severe inflammation.
  • Anti-inflammatory Medications: NSAIDs may help, but can worsen symptoms in some cases.
  • Leukotriene Receptor Antagonists: Block substances causing inflammation.
  • Immunomodulators: Drugs like omalizumab used for chronic cases.

Treatment: Traditional Medicine

  • Chamomile: Soothes itching with tea or compresses.
  • Calendula: Reduces redness using ointment.
  • Aloe Vera: Gel provides cooling relief.
  • Basil Leaves: Juice applied for soothing.

Home Remedies

  • Cold Compress: Reduces inflammation and itching.
  • Oatmeal Baths: Soothes skin irritation.
  • Baking Soda: Relieves itching with a paste or in bathwater.
  • Calamine Lotion: Provides a cooling sensation.

Caution

  • Avoid hot showers to prevent worsening itching.
  • Refrain from scratching to avoid skin irritation.
  • Identify and avoid potential triggers like certain foods, medications, and stress.

Prevention

  • Identify Triggers: Keep a diary to track potential triggers.
  • Avoid Allergens: Reduce exposure to known allergens.
  • Gradual Exposure: Under guidance, expose gradually to reduce sensitivity.
  • Stress Management: Use techniques like yoga or meditation.
