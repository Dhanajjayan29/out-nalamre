Warts

Overview

Warts are small, non-cancerous growths on the skin caused by the Human Papilloma Virus (HPV). They can appear on various parts of the body. Common warts typically develop on the hands and fingers, while plantar warts are found on the soles of the feet. Genital warts appear in the genital and anal areas.

Causes

Warts are contagious and can spread through direct contact or by touching surfaces contaminated with the virus. They are more common in children and people with weakened immune systems.

  • Direct contact with an infected person
  • Touching a wart and then another part of the body
  • Sharing personal items like towels or clothing
  • Some strains of HPV spread through sexual contact

Types of Warts

  • Plantar Warts: Found on the soles of the feet; often painful with tiny black dots.
  • Common Warts: Appear on hands and fingers; rough texture resembling cauliflower.
  • Flat Warts: Small, smooth warts on the face, neck, or legs; common in children.
  • Filiform Warts: Thread-like warts around the mouth, nose, or eyelids.
  • Genital Warts: Occur in the genital and anal areas; soft and moist in appearance.
  • Subungual and Periungual Warts: Found under or around the fingernails; can distort nail shape.

Symptoms

  • Small, rough bumps on the skin
  • Skin-colored or slightly darker than the surrounding area
  • Clustered appearance, known as "mosaic warts"
  • Pain or tenderness, especially for plantar warts
  • Black dots (clotted blood vessels)

Treatment: Modern Medicine

  • Over-the-counter treatments: Salicylic acid or freezing agents
  • Prescription medications: Higher concentration salicylic acid treatments
  • Cryotherapy: Freezing the wart with liquid nitrogen
  • Laser therapy: Using lasers to remove warts
  • Surgical removal: Cutting out the wart under local anesthesia

Treatment: Traditional Medicine

  • Duct tape occlusion: Covering the wart with duct tape to stimulate immune response
  • Apple cider vinegar: Applying vinegar to soften the wart
  • Garlic: Applying crushed garlic directly to the wart
  • Banana peel: Using banana peel over the wart to aid removal

Caution

  • Consult a healthcare professional before trying treatments
  • Avoid self-diagnosis; some growths may not be warts
  • Be patient; warts can take time to resolve
  • Follow instructions carefully to avoid skin damage
  • Avoid picking at warts to prevent spreading

Prevention

  • Practice good hygiene; wash hands regularly
  • Keep skin dry and clean
  • Avoid walking barefoot in public areas
  • Boost your immune system through a healthy lifestyle
