Scabies

Overview

Scabies is a contagious skin condition caused by a tiny mite called Sarcoptes scabiei. It is characterized by intense itching and a pimple-like rash. The mites burrow into the skin, where they lay eggs and reproduce, leading to characteristic symptoms. Scabies spreads through prolonged skin-to-skin contact with an infected person and can also spread through infested bedding, clothing, and furniture.

Causes

Scabies is highly contagious, and even brief skin-to-skin contact can be enough to transmit the mites. Other ways scabies can spread include:

  • Close Personal Contact
  • Household Contact
  • Infested Objects

Symptoms

  • Itchy red papules, occasionally vesicles, and pustules
  • Worse itching at night
  • Common rashes on palms, soles, wrists, axilla, umbilicus, and spaces between fingers
  • Linear or curved skin burrows may be observed
  • Itchy lumps or nodules in armpits and groin

Treatment: Modern Medicine

Medications:

  • Permethrin Cream
  • Lindane Lotion
  • Crotamiton Cream

Oral Medications:

  • Ivermectin
  • Antihistamines

Treatment: Traditional Medicine

  • Neem Oil
  • Tea Tree Oil
  • Sulfur Ointment
  • Clove Oil

Caution

  • Avoid Close Contact
  • Treat All Household Members
  • Avoid Scratching

Prevention

  • Avoid close contact with individuals who have scabies
  • Wash all clothing, bedding, and personal items in hot water
  • Vacuum the home thoroughly, including furniture and carpets
  • Inform close contacts to seek treatment if necessary
  • Maintain good personal hygiene, including regular handwashing
  • Avoid sharing personal items like towels and clothing
