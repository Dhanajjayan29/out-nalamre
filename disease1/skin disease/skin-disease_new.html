Scabies

Scabies is a contagious skin infestation caused by the microscopic mite *Sarcoptes scabiei*. These mites burrow into the top layer of the skin, where they lay eggs and cause intense itching.

Acne

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It primarily affects teenagers due to hormonal changes, but it can appear at any age.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that leads to the rapid growth of skin cells, forming thick, red, scaly patches, often on the scalp, elbows, and knees.

Eczema

Eczema is a condition that makes your skin red, inflamed, and itchy. It can occur anywhere on the body and is often triggered by irritants like allergens, soaps, or stress.

View more

Vitiligo

Vitiligo is a condition where the skin loses its pigment, resulting in patches of lighter skin. The exact cause is unknown, but it's often related to an autoimmune response.

View more

Melasma

Melasma is a common skin condition that causes brown or gray-brown patches, usually on the face. It is most commonly seen in women and is often triggered by hormonal changes or sun exposure.
