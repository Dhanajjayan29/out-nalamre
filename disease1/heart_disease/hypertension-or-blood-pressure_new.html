Hypertension or Blood Pressure

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a common condition where the force of the blood against the artery walls is consistently too high. If left untreated, it can lead to serious health problems such as heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage.

Causes and Risk Factors

The exact cause of hypertension is often unknown, but several factors can contribute to its development, including:

  • Age - Blood pressure tends to rise as you age.
  • Family history - If your parents or close relatives have high blood pressure, you may be at higher risk.
  • Obesity - Excess weight increases the strain on your heart.
  • Physical inactivity - Lack of exercise increases the risk of hypertension.
  • Excessive alcohol consumption or smoking.
  • Chronic stress.
  • Unhealthy diet, especially high salt intake.

Symptoms

High blood pressure often has no symptoms, which is why it is often called a "silent killer." However, in some cases, people with severely high blood pressure may experience:

  • Severe headaches.
  • Nausea or vomiting.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness.
  • Chest pain.

Treatment Options

Treatment for hypertension includes lifestyle changes and medications, such as:

Lifestyle Changes:
  • Regular physical activity.
  • A healthy diet, such as the DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet, rich in fruits, vegetables, and low-fat dairy products.
  • Limiting alcohol consumption and avoiding smoking.
  • Maintaining a healthy weight.
  • Managing stress.

Prevention Tips

To prevent hypertension, consider the following tips:

  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Eat a balanced diet, rich in potassium, and low in salt.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Avoid excessive alcohol and smoking.
  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly.
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques like yoga and meditation.
