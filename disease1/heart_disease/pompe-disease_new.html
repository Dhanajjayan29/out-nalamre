Pompe Disease

Pompe disease is a rare genetic disorder that causes muscle weakness and respiratory problems. It results from the accumulation of glycogen in the cells due to a deficiency in the enzyme acid alpha-glucosidase (GAA), which is responsible for breaking down glycogen.

Causes and Risk Factors

Pompe disease is inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern, which means that both parents must carry the mutated gene for a child to inherit the condition. The primary cause is a mutation in the GAA gene, which impairs the body's ability to break down glycogen.

Risk factors include:

  • Family history of Pompe disease.
  • Being born with a genetic mutation of the GAA gene.

Symptoms

The symptoms of Pompe disease can vary depending on the severity of the condition. Common symptoms include:

  • Muscle weakness, especially in the legs and trunk.
  • Breathing difficulties due to weakened respiratory muscles.
  • Enlarged heart (cardiomegaly).
  • Fatigue and difficulty with physical activities.

Treatment Options

Although there is no cure for Pompe disease, several treatments can help manage the symptoms and improve quality of life:

Enzyme Replacement Therapy (ERT):
  • ERT involves the intravenous infusion of the missing enzyme, acid alpha-glucosidase, to help break down glycogen in cells.
Supportive Care:
  • Physical therapy, respiratory support, and medications may help manage symptoms.

Prevention Tips

As Pompe disease is a genetic condition, prevention is not possible. However, early detection and treatment can help manage symptoms and improve outcomes. Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of Pompe disease.
