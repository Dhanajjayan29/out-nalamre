Coronary Artery Disease (Coronary Block)

Coronary artery disease (CAD) is a condition in which the coronary arteries, which supply blood to the heart, become narrowed or blocked. This can lead to chest pain, heart attacks, and other serious heart problems.

Causes and Risk Factors

Coronary artery disease is primarily caused by a buildup of fatty deposits (atherosclerosis) in the walls of the coronary arteries. Other causes and risk factors include:

  • High cholesterol levels.
  • High blood pressure (hypertension).
  • Diabetes or insulin resistance.
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Lack of physical activity and poor diet.
  • Family history of heart disease.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of coronary artery disease include:

  • Chest pain (angina), which may feel like pressure, tightness, or squeezing.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Fatigue and dizziness.
  • Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia).

Treatment Options

Treatment for coronary artery disease depends on the severity of the condition and may include:

Lifestyle Changes:
  • Eating a heart-healthy diet, exercising regularly, and quitting smoking can help prevent or manage CAD.
Medications:
  • Medications such as statins to lower cholesterol, beta-blockers to reduce blood pressure, and aspirin to prevent blood clots may be prescribed.
Surgical Interventions:
  • In severe cases, coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) or angioplasty may be required to improve blood flow to the heart.

Prevention Tips

The following lifestyle changes can help reduce the risk of coronary artery disease:

  • Maintain a healthy weight and eat a balanced, low-fat diet.
  • Exercise regularly and stay active.
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Control high blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels.
