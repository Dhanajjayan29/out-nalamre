Overview

Hemophilia is a genetic disorder characterized by a deficiency in certain blood clotting factors, specifically factors VIII or IX. This deficiency impairs the blood's ability to clot normally, leading to prolonged bleeding even from minor injuries. Hemophilia is typically inherited, meaning it's passed down through families, most often affecting males while females can be carriers of the gene.

There are different types of hemophilia:

  • Hemophilia A: Caused by a deficiency in clotting factor VIII.
  • Hemophilia B: Also known as Christmas disease, caused by a deficiency in clotting factor IX.
  • Haemophilia C: Results from low levels of factor XI.
  • Other types include Von Willebrand disease and parahaemophilia (low factor V).

Causes

Hemophilia is caused by a genetic mutation that affects the production of certain blood clotting factors. It is an inherited condition, primarily affecting males. Spontaneous mutations can also cause hemophilia, though this is rare. Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of hemophilia.

Symptoms

  • Prolonged bleeding: Difficulty in stopping bleeding after injuries.
  • Easy bruising: Large and slow-healing bruises from minor bumps.
  • Joint pain and swelling: Caused by bleeding into joints.
  • Muscle bleeding: Pain and swelling in affected areas.
  • Excessive bleeding from cuts: Difficult to control with standard first aid.
  • Blood in urine or stool: Possible gastrointestinal bleeding.
  • Nosebleeds: Recurring episodes, especially in severe cases.

Treatment: Modern Medicine

  • Replacement Therapy: Infusion of clotting factor concentrates.
  • Prophylactic Treatment: Regular infusions to prevent bleeding episodes.
  • On-Demand Treatment: Clotting factor infusions during bleeding episodes.
  • Gene Therapy: Emerging treatments to address genetic causes.
  • Hemostatic Agents: Medications to control bleeding.
  • Physical Therapy: To maintain joint health and mobility.

Treatment: Traditional Medicine

  • Herbal remedies: Use of anti-inflammatory herbs like arnica and turmeric.
  • Yoga and Tai Chi: Gentle exercises to improve flexibility.
  • Acupuncture: Techniques to reduce pain and inflammation.

Caution

  • Avoid high-risk activities: To prevent injuries and bleeding.
  • Medication Awareness: Avoid blood-thinning medications like aspirin.
  • Recognize Bleeding Signs: Includes easy bruising, joint pain, and blood in stool/urine.

Prevention

  • Regular Exercise: Strengthens muscles and joints.
  • Maintain a Healthy Weight: Reduces the risk of bleeding.
  • Vaccinations: Protects against infections.
  • Join Support Groups: For coping with the condition.
