Kidney Diseases

Kidney stones

A kidney stone is a hard deposit of minerals and salts that forms inside the kidneys. These stones can vary in size, ranging from as small as a grain of sand to as large as a golf ball.

Chronic Kidney Disease(CKD) or Kidney failure

CKD stands for Chronic Kidney Disease. It's a condition where your kidneys gradually lose function over time. This can happen for a variety of reasons, including high blood pressure.

Diabetes insipidus

Diabetes insipidus (DI) is a rare disorder characterized by excessive thirst and excretion of large amounts of diluted urine. It occurs when the kidneys are unable to properly concentrate urine.
