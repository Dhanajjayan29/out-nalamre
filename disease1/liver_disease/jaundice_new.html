Jaundice

Jaundice is a condition where the skin, sclerae (whites of the eyes), and mucous membranes turn yellow due to high levels of bilirubin in the blood. Bilirubin is a byproduct of the breakdown of red blood cells.

Causes and Risk Factors

Jaundice can be caused by a variety of conditions, including:

  • Hepatitis - An infection that causes inflammation of the liver.
  • Alcoholic liver disease - Liver damage caused by excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Gallstones - Blocks the bile ducts leading to the liver and gallbladder.
  • Cancer - Certain types of cancer can cause jaundice by blocking the bile ducts.

Risk factors for jaundice include:

  • Chronic liver diseases such as cirrhosis or hepatitis.
  • Excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Family history of liver disease or jaundice.
  • Older age and certain genetic factors.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of jaundice include:

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes (sclerae).
  • Dark-colored urine.
  • Clay-colored stools.
  • Fatigue and weakness.

Treatment Options

The treatment for jaundice depends on the underlying cause. Common treatments include:

Medications:
  • Medications to treat the underlying cause of jaundice, such as antivirals for hepatitis or antibiotics for infections.
Surgical Options:
  • Surgery to remove gallstones or tumors that are blocking the bile ducts.
  • Liver transplant in cases of severe liver failure.

Prevention Tips

To reduce the risk of jaundice, consider the following:

  • Limit alcohol consumption and avoid smoking.
  • Get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B.
  • Maintain a healthy weight and eat a balanced diet.
  • Regularly monitor liver health through medical check-ups.
