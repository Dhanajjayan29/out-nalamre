Fatty Liver

Fatty liver disease occurs when fat builds up in the liver cells. This condition can occur due to excessive alcohol consumption or other factors such as obesity, diabetes, and high cholesterol levels.

Causes and Risk Factors

Fatty liver disease can be caused by:

  • Alcohol consumption (Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease).
  • Obesity and being overweight.
  • Diabetes or insulin resistance.
  • High cholesterol and high triglyceride levels.
  • Genetics and family history of liver disease.

Risk factors include:

  • Excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Being overweight or obese.
  • Diabetes or metabolic syndrome.
  • High-fat or high-sugar diets.
  • Age (older individuals are at higher risk).

Symptoms

Common symptoms of fatty liver include:

  • Fatigue or feeling tired most of the time.
  • Unexplained weight loss.
  • Abdominal pain, especially on the upper right side.
  • Swollen abdomen or legs.

Treatment Options

The treatment for fatty liver disease may include:

Lifestyle Changes:
  • Weight loss through diet and exercise.
  • Limiting alcohol consumption.
  • Managing underlying conditions like diabetes and high cholesterol.
Medications:
  • There are no specific medications for fatty liver, but doctors may prescribe medications to treat underlying conditions such as diabetes or high cholesterol.

Prevention Tips

While fatty liver disease can be managed, the following lifestyle changes can help prevent or slow its progression:

  • Maintain a healthy weight through balanced diet and regular exercise.
  • Avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Monitor and control blood sugar, cholesterol, and triglyceride levels.
  • Eat a diet low in fats and sugar, and rich in fiber.
