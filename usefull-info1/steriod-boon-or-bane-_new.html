Steroid: Boon or Bane

Steroids, often prescribed for their anti-inflammatory and immune-suppressing properties, can be both beneficial and harmful depending on their usage.

  • Medical Benefits: Steroids are crucial in treating conditions like asthma, arthritis, and autoimmune diseases.
  • Anti-Inflammatory Effects: They help in reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms in many diseases.
  • Short-Term Use: When used under medical supervision for a short duration, steroids can be effective and relatively safe.
  • Risks of Misuse: Long-term use or misuse can lead to adverse effects on health.

Risks Associated with Steroid Use

  • Weight Gain: Steroid use can lead to weight gain and increased appetite.
  • Bone Density Loss: Prolonged use can weaken bones, increasing fracture risk.
  • Immune Suppression: Steroids can suppress the immune system, making the body more susceptible to infections.

  • Skin Changes: Long-term use can cause acne, thinning of skin, and easy bruising.
  • Organ Damage: High doses over long periods can damage the liver and kidneys.
  • Addiction Risk: Misuse of steroids can lead to psychological dependence.
  • Heart Risks: Increased risk of high blood pressure and cardiovascular issues with long-term use.
