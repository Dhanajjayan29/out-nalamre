Connection Between Diabetes and Blood Pressure

Diabetes and high blood pressure (hypertension) are two common health conditions that often coexist and can exacerbate each other. Here's an overview of their connection and associated risks.

  • Insulin Resistance: Individuals with type 2 diabetes often face insulin resistance, which can lead to an increase in blood pressure.
  • Damage to Blood Vessels: High blood sugar and high blood pressure can damage blood vessels, making them narrow and less flexible.

Complications and Risks

  • Heart Disease: High blood pressure can increase the risk of coronary artery disease and heart failure.
  • Stroke: Diabetes and hypertension significantly raise the likelihood of a stroke due to damaged blood vessels.
  • Kidney Damage: Combined effects can lead to kidney failure and other renal complications.
