The Issue of Antibiotic Misuse

Antibiotics are essential in fighting bacterial infections, but their misuse can lead to antibiotic resistance, making infections harder to treat. Let’s examine how misuse occurs and its impact.

  • Self-Medication: Many individuals use antibiotics without a doctor’s prescription, often for viral infections where they are ineffective.
  • Incomplete Courses: Not completing the full antibiotic course can leave some bacteria alive, which can become resistant.

Complications of Antibiotic Misuse

  • Increased Resistance: Bacteria exposed to antibiotics improperly can develop resistance, leading to "superbugs" that are harder to eliminate.
  • Side Effects: Unnecessary use of antibiotics can lead to adverse effects like allergic reactions, stomach issues, and more.
  • Global Health Threat: Antibiotic-resistant infections are projected to cause millions of deaths annually if misuse continues.
