Must Know Medical Terms

Understanding medical terminology is key to navigating the healthcare system. Here are some important medical terms to know:

  • Antibiotic: A medicine used to treat infections caused by bacteria. Antibiotics are not effective against viral infections such as the flu or common cold.
  • Analgesic: A drug that is used to relieve pain, including medications like acetaminophen (Tylenol) and ibuprofen (Advil).
  • Vaccine: A substance that stimulates the immune system to produce antibodies against specific diseases, helping to prevent infections.
  • Chronic: Refers to a condition that is persistent or long-lasting. Chronic conditions can last for months or even years, such as diabetes or arthritis.
  • Acute: Refers to a condition that comes on suddenly and is usually severe in nature, but typically lasts for a short period of time, like an acute infection or injury.

Common Medical Terms Explained

  • Hypertension: High blood pressure, which increases the risk of heart disease and stroke. It is often referred to as the "silent killer" because it may not show symptoms.
  • Diabetes: A group of diseases that affect how the body processes blood sugar (glucose). It can lead to complications such as heart disease, kidney failure, and blindness if not managed properly.
  • Anemia: A condition where there is a shortage of red blood cells or hemoglobin in the blood, leading to fatigue, weakness, and pallor.
  • Cardiovascular: Relating to the heart and blood vessels, commonly associated with conditions such as heart disease, heart attacks, and stroke.
  • Immunocompromised: A condition where the immune system is weakened, making the body more susceptible to infections. This can result from diseases like HIV or medications like chemotherapy.
