Rheumatoid Arthritis

  • Nalamaree Team
  • 25 April 2024

Overview

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is a chronic autoimmune disorder primarily affecting the joints, causing inflammation, pain, stiffness, and swelling. RA occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks the synovium, leading to inflammation and potential joint damage.

Causes

  • Genetic: Family history of RA may increase the risk.
  • Environmental triggers: Factors like infections or certain substances, including smoking, may trigger RA in genetically predisposed individuals.
  • Hormonal factors: Hormonal changes during pregnancy or menopause may influence RA, more common in women.
  • Immune system dysfunction: In RA, the immune system mistakenly targets the synovium, causing joint inflammation.

Symptoms

  • Joint pain: Persistent pain, typically affecting multiple joints.
  • Joint stiffness: Common in the morning or after periods of inactivity, improving with movement.
  • Joint swelling and tenderness: Inflammation causing swelling and tenderness in affected joints.
  • Reduced range of motion: Difficulty performing daily activities due to decreased joint mobility.
