Scabies

Scabies is a contagious skin condition caused by tiny mites that burrow into the skin, leading to intense itching and a rash.

View more

Acne

Acne is a skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil, dead skin cells, and sometimes bacteria, leading to pimples.

View more

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid skin cell turnover, leading to scaly, red patches on the skin.

View more

Leucoderma (Vitiligo)

Vitiligo is a condition where the skin loses its pigment, resulting in patches of lighter skin. The exact cause is unknown, but it's often related to an autoimmune response.

View more

Atopic Dermatitis (Eczema)

Eczema refers to a group of conditions that cause the skin to become inflamed, red, and itchy. It is often linked to allergies and asthma.

View more

Alopecia Areata (Spot Baldness)

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss in small, round patches on the scalp or other areas of the body.

View more

Hives (Urticaria)

Hives are raised, red, itchy welts that appear on the skin as a result of an allergic reaction or other triggers.

View more

Warts

Warts are small, non-cancerous growths on the skin caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV).

View more

Pruritus

Pruritus, commonly known as itching, is a sensation that provokes the desire to scratch, often due to dry skin, allergies, or other conditions.

View more

Infection (Bacterial, Fungal, Viral)

Infections caused by bacteria, fungi, or viruses can lead to a variety of skin issues, including rashes, pustules, and lesions.
